To the Prime Minister of Norway, Jonas Gahr Støre

Dear Prime Minister Støre, I am writing to express my concern regarding the urgent need for greater action on climate change in Norway. While our nation has made significant progress in sustainability, we must continue to innovate and implement more effective measures to address this global challenge. One area where I believe we can make a substantial impact is in managing food waste. Despite Norway’s advanced waste management systems, a significant amount of edible food is still wasted, contributing to unnecessary greenhouse gas emissions. By introducing stronger policies and public awareness initiatives, we can reduce this waste and promote a more sustainable food system. Additionally, improving our recycling systems is essential for creating a circular economy. Increasing access to recycling facilities, expanding the types of materials that can be recycled, and investing in new technologies can help us move closer to achieving zero waste. Lastly, I urge the government to continue supporting green technologies and renewable energy projects. These initiatives not only combat climate change but also drive economic growth and create jobs. By leading in these areas, Norway can set an inspiring example for other nations to follow. Thank you for your attention to this critical issue. I trust that under your leadership, Norway will continue to make significant strides toward a sustainable and resilient future.

Sincerely, Peter Stoltz
